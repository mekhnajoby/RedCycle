RedCycle

Reuse-first recycling for a Mars habitat at Jezero Crater

A prototype that models inventory, a waste pool, modular processing, mass-balance, and spatial planning.

Challenge & Objectives

  • Design a system to manage and reuse inorganic waste on Mars (Jezero Crater).
  • Maximize resource recovery, minimize energy/water use and unusable outputs.
  • Focus on immediate reuse and in-situ resource utilization (ISRU).

What we built

  • Inventory & waste pool (reuse-first policy)
  • Processing modules (Quick / Full) + secondary recovery
  • Hud: recovered vs wasted, energy/water tradeoffs, Eco modes
  • Map: per-material centroids and location drill-down

Scenario Workflows

Residence Renovations: reclaim aluminum & foam to build fixtures and insulation.

Cosmic Celebrations: reuse textiles & pouches for decorations and small containers.

Daring Discoveries: recover carbon and refurbish filters for experimental reuse.

How it reduces inputs & waste

  • Consume wastePool before fresh inventory — fewer new inputs.
  • Secondary recovery extracts extra mass from residues.
  • Eco/Ultra Eco modes lower water/energy, trading intensity for efficiency.

Demo walkthrough (quick)

  1. Drag inventory items to a Module Panel to stage material.
  2. Choose Quick or Full processing; observe HUD updates.
  3. Open the map and click a legend type to show locations and centroids.
